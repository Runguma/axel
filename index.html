    NATIONAL AND INTERNATIONAL SETTING

    Most infrastructure and services are concentrated in SC and SW Kenya where the majority of the Kenyan population lives.

    Rapid urbanization and urban growth mean that rural-urban connectivity is becoming increasingly important, requires investment. Drivers of urbanization: transport/infrastructure corridors, econ. activities, econ. potential, presence of natural resources, admin. functions. Kenya is one of fastest growing econ. in Africa, but wealth is not evenly distributed with 90% of Kenyans in the bottom 40% of the income bracket living in rural areas.

    RELEVANT NATIONAL PLANNING FRAMEWORKS

    Turkana County:LAPSSET could greatly increase development pressure on Turkana County meaning that proactive planning will need to be be conducted in order to guide sustainable development in line with population growth, urbanisation and land and resource management

    Garissa County:Garissa is considered a ‘gateway town’ whose functionality and livability needs to be enhanced.

    CLIMATE RISK CONTEXT

    Kenya ranks 150th out of 181 countries in terms of vulnerability to climate change; ASAL counties such as Turkana are particularly at risk. Climate issues have caused severe crop and livestock losses, famine and population displacement. Climate change in surrounding countries such as South Sudan also impacts displacement trends. Planning needs to not only take climate into account, but actively mitigate and adapt to climate change.

    DISPLACEMENT DYNAMICS IN KENYA

    There are 494,000 refugees in Kenya, predominantly from Somalia (54%) and South Sudan (25%).

    Turkana: Due to proximity, refugees in Turkana County are predominantly from South Sudan, and DRC.

    Dadaab: Before the large-scale influx of refugees in early 1990s and the establishment of Dadaab in 1991, integration policies were mainly locally-led; they are now more centrally led with a focus on refugee camps (although Kalobeyei was established as an integretad settlement)

    About us

    This programme aims to utilise Urban and Spatial Planning as a tool by which to bridge the gap between humanitarian and development activities. UN-Habitat headquarters, regional and country offices, and several partners deploy a wide range of planning projects and tools in different countries to bring together diverse solutions to address the commitments within the Global Compact on Refugees, Global Compact for Migration, Agenda 2030 and the Sustainable Development Goals, which include exploring “Alternatives to Camps”, supporting the paradigm shift towards the integration of refugee and host communities in combined programming, and development of sustainable and long-term solutions as part of multi-actor and comprehensive responses to “Leave No One Behind”, ensuring the most vulnerable populations are promoted and protected..
